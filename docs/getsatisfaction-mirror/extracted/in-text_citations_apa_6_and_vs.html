In-Text Citations (APA 6) 'and' vs. '&'

Hi -

According to APA 6, given multiple authors, if the authors are separated from the year in an in-text citation then 'and' should be used. Like this:

Rand and Collins (1994)

When the authors are not separated from the year then an '&' should be used, like this:

(Rand & Collins, 1994)

Qiqqa does not handle the first case correctly. It renders it with an '&'. I dug through the CSL file, but there isn't anything that I can see that allows me to specific the distinction between the two cases (only attributes to change both cases from an '&' to 'and'.)

Is this something that can be filtered in Qiqqa's code?

Thanks,

    Dennis over 6 years ago
    First, you can't do it in CSL.

    It will require a rewrite of Qiqqa to get the functionality you want.

    Other citation managers (Zotero, Mendeley, etc.) that use the same citation processor engine offer a "suppress author" option.  This leaves the author out of the parentheses of the citation, and the author name(s), which are now part of the sentence, are typed in manually.  This has the added bonus that if you ever change styles to one using numeric citations, the sentence still makes sense.

    Finally, you mentioned changing "internal programming".  If you are comfortable writing javascript, you can modify the script "run_incite.js".
