Inserting multiple references in a word document.

Hello, when I insert multiple references in vancouver style with Incite, they are all inserted individually, for example: "Based on the literature (1)(2,3)(4) ..."
Is there a way to make then look like: " Based on the literature (1-4)..." ?

    andi over 8 years ago
    How do you insert the references?

    If you tick all the check boxes of the papers in InCite you want to cite and press "add citation" once, it will do what you want: (1-4).

    If you insert each citation individually, one after the other, InCite will produce individual citations like (1)(2,3)(4).

    So make sure you add all citations on the given spot at the same time by marking them in InCite and pressing "add citation" only once.

    As I replied in your other thread, you most probably did not reformat the document by pressing the "magic wand" button. What you see within the brackets are the bibtex keys or qiqqa keys which InCite uses internally. These will be changed into the numbered references by the "magic wand" or "reformat document" button.
